Lockwood STEM | PLTW Engineering

Principles of Engineering

An aerospace-focused engineering course centered on systems thinking, mechanisms, energy, structures, electronics, controls, testing, fabrication, and mission-ready design.

InstructorJonathan Lockwood
Emailjlockwood@cornerstonecharter.com
Course Level10th–11th Grade | Full Year

What You’ll Learn

POE uses aerospace applications to study how engineers design, test, and improve systems. You will analyze aircraft and spacecraft systems, build mechanisms, evaluate structures and energy transfer, create sensor-based control systems, and document engineering decisions with evidence.

Course Schedule

Class Period

45-minute class periods focused on short instruction, project work, testing, cleanup, and notebook evidence.

Daily Evidence

Every class should produce a sketch, screenshot, photo, calculation, test result, diagram, code evidence, revision note, or reflection.

What You’ll Work On

Unit 0

Product Design and Development
Design a laser-cut leather or approved flexible-material product inspired by flight crew gear, aerospace field kits, mission organization, and human-centered product development.

Unit 1

Aerospace Systems and Engineering Design
Analyze aerospace systems using inputs, outputs, constraints, failure modes, feedback, tradeoffs, and documentation.

Unit 2

Mechanisms, Motion, and Flight Control
Apply mechanical advantage, linkages, gears, cams, pulleys, and motion transfer to aerospace mechanisms such as control surfaces, landing gear, hatches, and deployment systems.

Unit 3

Energy, Power, and Aerospace Structures
Investigate energy conversion, power, efficiency, load paths, material behavior, trusses, wings, fuselages, and structural testing.

Unit 4

Avionics, Control Systems, and Automation
Use circuits, sensors, actuators, code, logic, data, and troubleshooting to model feedback-controlled aerospace systems.

Major Projects & Design Challenges

Mission Gear Leather Goods Challenge

Design, CAD model, laser cut, prototype, test, revise, and fabricate a functional aerospace-inspired soft-goods product.

Aircraft Systems Investigation

Analyze a real aircraft or spacecraft subsystem by mapping inputs, outputs, constraints, tradeoffs, feedback, and failure points.

Flight Control Mechanism Challenge

Design and test a mechanism that transfers or transforms motion like an aerospace control surface, landing gear, hatch, or deployment system.

Aerospace Energy and Structures Challenge

Build or model a system that demonstrates energy conversion, power, efficiency, structural loading, or strength-to-weight performance.

Avionics Control System Challenge

Create a sensor-based control system that models an aerospace feedback loop such as attitude sensing, landing indicator, payload release, or automated alert.

PLTW End-of-Course Exam

You will complete the official PLTW End-of-Course (EOC) Exam at the conclusion of the course. The EOC Exam will count as 100 points toward the final course grade.

Late Work, Revisions & Make-Up Work

Late Work

Assignments submitted late will receive a 10% deduction for each day late.

Revisions

Tests and quizzes may not be revised. Classwork, design challenges, and performance tasks may be revised for additional credit.

Absences

You receive one class period to make up work for each class period missed.

To be eligible for full credit through revision, the original submission must be turned in on time. Late initial submissions may still be accepted, but they are not eligible to earn full credit through revision.

Your Responsibility as an Engineer

All your work must represent original effort and authentic engineering thinking. You must be able to explain your sketches, calculations, CAD files, code, data, and design decisions.

AI Use Policy: You may use AI tools for brainstorming, idea generation, research support, troubleshooting, and revision support. AI may not be used to generate complete solutions, complete designs, final CAD work, final code, fabricated submissions, or final written responses.

Course Philosophy

Success in this course requires persistence, collaboration, careful documentation, and a willingness to improve through testing, feedback, and iteration.
