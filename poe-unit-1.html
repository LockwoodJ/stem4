Aerospace Engineering Focus

Every unit connects engineering principles to aircraft, spacecraft, drones, launch systems, avionics, structures, and mission-ready design.

Unit Focus

Aircraft and Spacecraft Systems — Students use aerospace applications to make the engineering principles concrete, testable, and connected to real design decisions.

Understand

Aerospace Concepts

  • Inputs, processes, outputs, and feedback
  • Subsystems in aircraft and spacecraft
  • Constraints, tradeoffs, and failure modes
  • Mission requirements and design briefs
  • Systems diagrams and technical communication
Apply

Technical Skills

  • Map an aircraft or spacecraft subsystem
  • Create black-box and detailed system diagrams
  • Identify risks and design tradeoffs
  • Support claims with evidence
  • Write a clear technical explanation
Document

Engineering Evidence

  • Aerospace system diagram
  • Subsystem function table
  • Failure mode analysis
  • Design brief with criteria/constraints
  • Systems investigation reflection

Student Deliverables

  • Dated engineering notebook entries with sketches, data, screenshots, photos, calculations, and reflections.
  • Technical work showing aerospace context, design decisions, and evidence-based revision.
  • Prototype, model, diagram, test result, or analysis artifact connected to the unit focus.
  • Final reflection explaining what changed, why it changed, and how the evidence supports the decision.
