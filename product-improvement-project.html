Project Overview

In this project, you will practice user-centered design by evaluating an existing product, identifying a meaningful opportunity for improvement, developing redesigned concepts, and justifying your final solution with evidence.

Project Focus

Use these focus areas to guide your notebook, design decisions, and final reflection.

User-Centered Design

Design for People

Identify real user needs and make design choices that improve the user experience.

Product Analysis

Understand the Existing Design

Study how the current product works, where it succeeds, and where it could improve.

Redesign

Improve with Evidence

Use feedback, testing, or comparison to support your proposed improvement.

Criteria and Constraints

Your design must meet the project requirements while showing thoughtful engineering decisions.

  • The redesigned product must address a clear user need or product weakness.
  • The improvement must be realistic and clearly explained.
  • Sketches or models must show how the redesign changes the original product.
  • The design must consider usability, function, materials, and constraints.
  • Final recommendations must be supported by evidence or reasoning.

Suggested Workflow

Use this sequence to stay organized from the first idea to final submission.

  1. Choose Product: Select a product with a clear opportunity for improvement.
  2. Analyze: Identify user needs, weaknesses, and constraints.
  3. Ideate: Sketch several redesign concepts.
  4. Select: Choose the strongest concept and justify it.
  5. Develop: Create detailed drawings, CAD, or prototype evidence.
  6. Present: Explain the improvement and support it with evidence.

Rubric

Projects are evaluated on process, evidence, technical quality, and reflection.

Problem Definition 15 pts

Clearly identifies the problem, user needs, criteria, and constraints.

Planning and Documentation 20 pts

Notebook shows sketches, design decisions, revisions, and process evidence.

Technical Execution 25 pts

Final design or model is accurate, complete, functional, and carefully made.

Testing and Iteration 20 pts

Uses evidence from testing or feedback to improve the design.

Final Reflection 10 pts

Explains what changed, what worked, and what could be improved.

Professionalism 10 pts

Uses class time, tools, materials, and teamwork responsibly.
