Project Overview

This Unit 0 challenge introduces product development through a small, useful, aerospace-inspired soft-goods product produced with CAD and laser cutting.

Focus

Engineering Design

Students define a mission or crew need, set criteria and constraints, generate concepts, prototype, test, revise, and defend design decisions.

CAD

Flat Pattern Modeling

Students create a laser-ready 2D pattern with cut lines, fold/score lines, stitch holes, hardware holes, and dimensions.

Make

Digital Manufacturing

Students laser cut prototypes and a final product, then assemble using stitching, rivets, snaps, lacing, adhesive, or hardware.

Design Challenge Prompt

You have been hired as a product designer developing a new line of aerospace-inspired mission gear. Your task is to design and build a functional leather or approved flexible-material product for a specific user, crew role, classroom mission, or field use case.

Your product must solve a real problem, demonstrate thoughtful pattern design, and show evidence of testing and iteration. You will analyze existing leather goods, mission gear, field kits, and aerospace soft-goods, develop your own CAD pattern, laser cut prototypes, revise your design, and fabricate a final leather product.

Driving Question

How do engineers and product designers turn material, manufacturing, user, and mission constraints into useful products?

Who is the product for, and what aerospace or mission need does it support?
What problem does it solve?
How does the pattern support function and assembly?
What changed because of testing?

Project Requirements

Projects should remain small enough to complete in class while still showing real product design decisions.

Final Product Must

  • Be designed as a CAD-based flat pattern.
  • Be cut using the laser cutter.
  • Be made primarily from approved leather or laser-safe leather-like material.
  • Fit within one 12 in × 12 in sheet unless approved.
  • Include at least one functional feature such as a pocket, fold, closure, strap, loop, sleeve, or holder.
  • Include at least one joining method: stitching, rivets, snaps, lacing, adhesive, or hardware.
  • Show at least one documented design iteration from prototype to final product.

Recommended Aerospace-Inspired Product Types

  • Flight crew badge holder, card sleeve, or minimalist wallet
  • Keychain, luggage tag, or badge holder
  • Cord organizer or earbud wrap
  • Pencil sleeve, glasses sleeve, or notebook cover
  • Dice pouch, snap pouch, tool roll, or small belt-loop accessory

Approval required: large bags, complex wallets, belts, zipper pouches, or anything requiring advanced assembly.

100-Point Project Rubric

The final grade emphasizes process evidence, CAD quality, iteration, and communication—not just the finished product.

Engineering Notebook Entries
30 pts

Daily entries are complete, specific, and include meaningful evidence such as sketches, screenshots, photos, data, design decisions, and reflection.

Product Analysis and Design Definition
10 pts

Student analyzes existing products, defines a target user, writes a clear problem statement, and establishes appropriate criteria and constraints.

Concept Development and Selection
10 pts

Student develops multiple viable concepts, labels design features, and uses a decision matrix to justify the selected concept.

CAD Pattern Development
15 pts

CAD pattern is accurate, organized, dimensioned, and laser-ready with cut lines, fold/score lines, stitch holes, hardware holes, and clear pattern logic.

Prototyping and Iteration
15 pts

Student completes prototypes, tests fit/function/construction, documents feedback, and makes specific CAD revisions based on evidence.

Final Product Quality
10 pts

Final product is functional, assembled with care, meets constraints, and demonstrates appropriate use of material and manufacturing methods.

Product Pitch and Reflection
10 pts

Presentation clearly explains the user need, design process, testing, iteration, final product, and future improvements.

Final Submission Checklist

  • Engineering notebook entries 0.1–0.20
  • Concept sketches and decision matrix
  • Prototype 1 evidence and test report
  • CAD revision log
  • Prototype 2 evidence and final design review
  • Final CAD/vector file
  • Photos of final product
  • Product pitch and final reflection
