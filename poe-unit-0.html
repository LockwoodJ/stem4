Principles of Engineering

Unit 0: Product Design and Development

Design a laser-cut leather or approved flexible-material product inspired by flight crew gear, aerospace field kits, mission organization, and human-centered product development.

Aerospace Engineering Focus

Every unit connects engineering principles to aircraft, spacecraft, drones, launch systems, avionics, structures, and mission-ready design.

Unit Focus

Mission Gear Soft-Goods Challenge — Students use aerospace applications to make the engineering principles concrete, testable, and connected to real design decisions.

Understand

Aerospace Concepts

  • Human-centered aerospace product design
  • Soft-goods pattern development
  • CAD flat patterns for laser cutting
  • Material constraints, fasteners, fold lines, and edge quality
  • Prototype testing and iteration
Apply

Technical Skills

  • Analyze mission gear and product design choices
  • Create CAD cut/score/engrave layers
  • Laser cut prototype patterns
  • Document daily engineering notebook entries
  • Pitch a finished aerospace-inspired product
Document

Engineering Evidence

  • Product observation gallery walk
  • User/problem/criteria/constraints entry
  • Concept sketches and decision matrix
  • Prototype 1 and 2 fabrication logs
  • Final CAD pattern, final product, product reflection

Student Deliverables

  • Dated engineering notebook entries with sketches, data, screenshots, photos, calculations, and reflections.
  • Technical work showing aerospace context, design decisions, and evidence-based revision.
  • Prototype, model, diagram, test result, or analysis artifact connected to the unit focus.
  • Final reflection explaining what changed, why it changed, and how the evidence supports the decision.

Project Connection

This unit connects to the Mission Gear Leather Goods Challenge. Use the project page to understand the challenge, constraints, deliverables, testing expectations, and rubric.
