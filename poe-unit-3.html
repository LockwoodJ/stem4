Aerospace Engineering Focus

Every unit connects engineering principles to aircraft, spacecraft, drones, launch systems, avionics, structures, and mission-ready design.

Unit Focus

Propulsion, Power, and Airframe Performance — Students use aerospace applications to make the engineering principles concrete, testable, and connected to real design decisions.

Understand

Aerospace Concepts

  • Energy conversion and power
  • Efficiency and losses
  • Lift, thrust, drag, and weight as design constraints
  • Structural loading and load paths
  • Material selection and airframe performance
Apply

Technical Skills

  • Calculate work, power, and efficiency
  • Build and test a lightweight structure
  • Collect data from trials
  • Compare strength-to-weight performance
  • Use data to improve a design
Document

Engineering Evidence

  • Energy or structure calculations
  • Prototype test setup
  • Trial data table
  • Failure analysis photos
  • Design revision summary

Student Deliverables

  • Dated engineering notebook entries with sketches, data, screenshots, photos, calculations, and reflections.
  • Technical work showing aerospace context, design decisions, and evidence-based revision.
  • Prototype, model, diagram, test result, or analysis artifact connected to the unit focus.
  • Final reflection explaining what changed, why it changed, and how the evidence supports the decision.
