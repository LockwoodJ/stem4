Unit Overview

Apply user-centered design to analyze existing products, identify improvement opportunities, and redesign with purpose.

Unit Focus

Use these focus areas to guide your notebook entries, classwork, project planning, and reflection.

User Needs

Design for People

Identify who the product is for and what problem the redesign should solve.

Product Analysis

Study Existing Designs

Evaluate function, usability, materials, strengths, and weaknesses.

Redesign

Improve with Evidence

Use research, feedback, and comparison to justify design improvements.

Skills and Deliverables

This unit builds the technical and documentation skills needed for successful project work.

Key Skills

  • User research
  • Product analysis
  • Concept generation
  • Design justification
  • Redesign modeling
  • Feedback and reflection

Evidence to Produce

  • Product analysis
  • User need statement
  • Redesign sketches
  • CAD or prototype evidence
  • Improvement justification
  • Final reflection

Suggested Workflow

Follow this sequence to move from learning to application and final reflection.

01
Select

Choose a product with a meaningful improvement opportunity.

02
Analyze

Identify weaknesses, user needs, and design constraints.

03
Ideate

Generate multiple redesign concepts.

04
Develop

Create a detailed redesign through sketches, CAD, or prototype evidence.

05
Justify

Explain how the redesign better meets user needs.
