Project Overview

In this project, you will work with an assembly-based design problem. You will modify and improve rocket components, consider how parts fit together, document tolerances, and use testing evidence to support design changes.

Project Focus

Use these focus areas to guide your notebook, design decisions, and final reflection.

Assembly Design

Parts Working Together

Design parts that fit, align, and function as a complete system.

Tolerances

Fit and Function

Consider how small changes in dimensions affect performance and assembly.

Testing

Evidence-Based Improvement

Use launch data and observations to justify design revisions.

Criteria and Constraints

Your design must meet the project requirements while showing thoughtful engineering decisions.

  • Rocket body must safely fit the launcher system.
  • Fins and components must be securely attached or integrated.
  • Design changes must be documented in CAD and in the engineering notebook.
  • The rocket must be tested safely under instructor direction.
  • Final improvements must be supported by testing evidence.

Suggested Workflow

Use this sequence to stay organized from the first idea to final submission.

  1. Analyze System: Identify how the rocket, fins, body, and launcher interact.
  2. Model Components: Modify or create CAD parts with attention to fit.
  3. Assemble: Check part relationships and constraints.
  4. Test: Launch and collect performance evidence.
  5. Revise: Improve based on observed results.
  6. Submit: Turn in CAD evidence, test notes, and reflection.

Rubric

Projects are evaluated on process, evidence, technical quality, and reflection.

Problem Definition 15 pts

Clearly identifies the problem, user needs, criteria, and constraints.

Planning and Documentation 20 pts

Notebook shows sketches, design decisions, revisions, and process evidence.

Technical Execution 25 pts

Final design or model is accurate, complete, functional, and carefully made.

Testing and Iteration 20 pts

Uses evidence from testing or feedback to improve the design.

Final Reflection 10 pts

Explains what changed, what worked, and what could be improved.

Professionalism 10 pts

Uses class time, tools, materials, and teamwork responsibly.
