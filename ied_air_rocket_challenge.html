IED Engineering Challenge

Air Rocket Design Challenge

Design and build an air rocket that launches safely, flies consistently, and demonstrates thoughtful assembly design through proper fits, fin design, testing, and iteration.

View Challenge Brief View 100-Point Rubric
Project Type Assembly Design Challenge
Estimated Length 5 Class Periods
Final Product Launch-Ready Air Rocket
Total Points 100 Points

Challenge Brief

Your team will modify a provided rocket body design and create a unique fin system. The goal is not just to make a rocket that launches, but to engineer an assembly that fits correctly, performs consistently, and improves through testing.

Design Goal

  • Create an air rocket that launches safely and consistently.
  • Design a unique fin system that improves stability.
  • Use proper press-fit and slip-fit assembly techniques.
  • Test, evaluate, and revise your design before final launch day.

Design Constraints

Your solution must meet every required constraint.

Rocket Body

  • Start with the provided rocket body design.
  • Modify the design only where allowed by the project requirements.
  • The body must use a slip-fit over the pneumatic launcher.

Fin Assembly

  • Create one unique fin design.
  • Fins must attach using a press-fit.
  • Fin geometry should support stable flight.

Launch Performance

  • Rocket must launch safely.
  • Rocket should fly consistently across multiple tests.
  • Design changes should be based on test results.

Engineering Focus

  • Assembly design and how parts connect.
  • Fit types, including press-fit and slip-fit connections.
  • Stability, drag, and how fin geometry affects flight.
  • Testing and iteration based on observed performance.

Safety Expectations

  • Only launch when instructed by the teacher.
  • Never aim rockets at people, windows, or equipment.
  • Stand clear of the launch area during testing.
  • Damaged rockets must be repaired before relaunching.

FabLab Fabrication

  • 3D printing is allowed for certified students.
  • Students who are not certified must submit through the FabLab Fabrication page.
  • Fabricated parts must be measured, labeled, and documented.
  • All parts must support the rocket’s performance and assembly goals.

Fabrication Reminder

A better fabricated part does not automatically make a better design. Your grade depends on how well the part fits, functions, supports your design goal, and improves after testing.

Required Deliverables

Each team must submit evidence of both the design process and the final rocket.

  1. Engineering notebook documentation showing sketches, design decisions, testing, and iteration.
  2. Initial rocket and fin concept sketches with labels and notes.
  3. Final fin design with explanation of how it supports stable flight.
  4. Evidence of press-fit fin assembly and slip-fit rocket body design.
  5. Testing notes showing launch observations and design improvements.
  6. Final launch-ready rocket and reflection on performance.

Suggested Project Timeline

This timeline may be adjusted based on class progress and fabrication needs.

Day 1Challenge introduction, safety expectations, rocket body review, and concept sketching.
Day 2CAD modifications, unique fin design, fit planning, and fabrication preparation.
Day 3Build, fabricate, assemble, and check press-fit and slip-fit connections.
Day 4Launch testing, data collection, observation notes, and design iteration.
Day 5Final launch evaluation, notebook completion, and reflection submission.

100-Point Rubric

Your grade is based on design process, assembly quality, performance, documentation, and teamwork.

Category Points Excellent Performance
Design Process 20 Team clearly follows the engineering design process through sketching, planning, building, testing, and iteration. Design decisions are thoughtful and supported by evidence.
Assembly Design 15 Rocket demonstrates accurate assembly design. Press-fit fins and slip-fit body are functional, secure, and appropriate for the launcher and flight goals.
Flight Performance 15 Rocket launches safely and performs consistently across multiple tests. Flight path shows reasonable stability and design control.
Unique Fin Design 10 Fin design is original, well-planned, and connected to stability goals. Geometry is intentional rather than decorative.
Build Quality and Craftsmanship 10 Final rocket is clean, durable, well-assembled, and carefully finished. Parts fit together without excessive looseness, force, or damage.
Testing and Iteration 10 Team tests the rocket, records observations, identifies problems, and makes meaningful improvements before final launch.
Sketches and Technical Documentation 10 Sketches, notes, CAD evidence, and fit decisions are clearly documented. Work shows how the design developed over time.
Safety and Professionalism 5 Team follows launch safety expectations, uses tools appropriately, and treats the testing environment professionally.
Teamwork and Use of Class Time 5 Team shares responsibilities, communicates effectively, and uses class time productively throughout the project.
