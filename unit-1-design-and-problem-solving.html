Overview Focus Skills Workflow Project

Unit Overview

Learn how engineers define problems, identify criteria and constraints, generate ideas, test solutions, and communicate design decisions.

Unit Focus

Use these focus areas to guide your notebook entries, classwork, project planning, and reflection.

Design Process

Define the Problem

Use criteria, constraints, research, and stakeholder needs to frame engineering problems clearly.

Communication

Sketch and Document

Use sketches, notes, photos, and written explanations to make thinking visible.

Iteration

Test and Improve

Use evidence and feedback to revise designs and explain improvements.

Skills and Deliverables

This unit builds the technical and documentation skills needed for successful project work.

Key Skills

  • Problem definition
  • Criteria and constraints
  • Brainstorming and sketching
  • Decision making
  • Prototype planning
  • Testing and reflection

Evidence to Produce

  • Engineering notebook entries
  • Concept sketches
  • Design decision evidence
  • Prototype or model evidence
  • Testing notes
  • Final reflection

Suggested Workflow

Follow this sequence to move from learning to application and final reflection.

01
Launch

Analyze the design problem and identify criteria and constraints.

02
Explore

Research examples, sketch ideas, and compare possible solutions.

03
Develop

Choose a direction and create a plan for building or modeling.

04
Test

Use feedback or performance evidence to evaluate the design.

05
Improve

Revise the solution and explain what changed.
