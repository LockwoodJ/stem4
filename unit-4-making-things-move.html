Unit 4

Overview Focus Skills Workflow Project

Unit Overview

Explore mechanisms and motion by designing systems that transform input motion into controlled, repeatable output motion.

Unit Focus

Use these focus areas to guide your notebook entries, classwork, project planning, and reflection.

Mechanisms

Create Motion

Use cams, gears, linkages, cranks, and other mechanisms to produce movement.

Motion Planning

Control the Output

Predict and document how input motion becomes a desired output motion.

Mechanical Design

Build Reliable Systems

Design systems that move smoothly, repeatably, and safely.

Skills and Deliverables

This unit builds the technical and documentation skills needed for successful project work.

Key Skills

  • Mechanism analysis
  • Cams and linkages
  • Motion diagrams
  • Mechanical sketching
  • CAD modeling
  • Testing and adjustment

Evidence to Produce

  • Mechanism sketches
  • Motion planning notes
  • CAD or prototype evidence
  • Testing observations
  • Adjustment notes
  • Final reflection

Suggested Workflow

Follow this sequence to move from learning to application and final reflection.

01
Explore

Study how mechanisms convert motion.

02
Plan

Sketch the intended motion and mechanical system.

03
Model

Create parts or assemblies that support movement.

04
Build

Construct and test the mechanism.

05
Refine

Adjust the system for smoother or more reliable motion.
